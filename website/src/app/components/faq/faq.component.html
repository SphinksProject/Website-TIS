FAQ

  • What are open and decentralized cryptocurrencies and blockchains; how do they function and what are the benefits?

    • Cryptocurrencies are tradeable digital tokens with a price determined by supply & demand based on its value proposition. A blockchain is essentially the digital ledger on which the tokenized transactions are recorded.

    A blockchain is more aptly referred to as a Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT) due to the decentralized nature of how transactions are recorded and verified. Blocks of transaction data are added to the ledger on a continual and predetermined basis by anybody who is willing to direct their computer resources to process the data (these computers are referred to as nodes).

    As there is no central authority to whom we must trust to issue the true version of a blockchain, the network must come to a majority consensus on what is true (various types of consensus mechanisms exist to achieve this). Legitimate transactions are those that most users on the network agree are valid blocks, while non-valid blocks are discarded by the network. When a node submits a block which the network agrees is valid and therefore continues to build upon, the nodes efforts in processing the data is financially rewarded (usually with transaction fees/newly created tokens). If a node submits a block that contains intentionally false transaction data, it is rejected by the network and the nodes efforts in creating the block is lost (along with any other assets involved in the consensus mechanism). These mechanisms incentivize nodes to act honestly in processing valid transactions.

    A properly decentralized system means that there are no central servers or systems to trust or rely upon as almost anyone can process data for the network. An open system means that the code is entirely visible and therefore checkable and usable by anyone. This open and decentralized combination is a major part of what gives every user the power over their own funds. Such systems are capable of transferring value of any amount, anywhere across the globe, at any time of any day and verified as virtually immutable within an hour (which is a major advancement to the current banking system).
